We knew the world would not be the same. Few people laughed. Few people cried. Most people were silent. I remembered the line from the Hindu scripture, the Bhagavad Gita. Vishnu is trying to persuade the prince that he should do his duty, and to impress him, takes on his multi-armed form and says, "Now I am become Death, the destroyer of worlds." I suppose we all thought that, one way or another.

1945: America dropping the bombs “Little Boy” and “Fat Man” on the Japanese cities of Hiroshima and Nagasaki in WWII with devastating effects on the residents of the region. The goal was to get Japan to surrender, which was successful.
1957: The British nuclear reactor of Windscale, purposed to produce plutonium for atomic bombs, had a fire, which lead to radiation being spread through the UK and Europe.
1968: The treaty of Non-Proliferation of nuclear weapons (NPT) was signed. It’s purpose is to prevent the spread of nuclear weapons and their technology, and to achieve complete nuclear disarmament.
1986: The core of the soviet nuclear reactor Chernobyl melted, which lead to several explosions and fires. This released huge amounts of radiation into the air which spread all over Europe. It is called one of the worst civilian nuclear accidents, second only to Fukushima.
1991: A treaty signed by the US and the Soviet Union, which barred both parties of deploying nuclear weapons.
2007: The uranium prices exploded following China building multiple new nuclear reactors.
2011: In 2011, an earthquake happened in Japan, which triggered a tsunami, which in turn lead to several meltdowns and explosions in the nuclear reactor of Fukushima. This was the worst civilian nuclear disaster.
